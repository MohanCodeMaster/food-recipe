Recipe Revel




Ingredients

  • Toor dal (pigeon peas): 1 cup
  • Turmeric powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Tamarind pulp: 1 tablespoon (or a small lemon-sized ball of tamarind soaked in water)
  • Mixed vegetables: 2 cups (carrots, drumsticks, potatoes, eggplant, pumpkin, okra, etc.), cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Tomatoes: 2 medium, chopped
  • Onions or shallots: 1 medium, chopped (optional)
  • Green chilies: 2, slit
  • Sambar powder: 2 tablespoons
  • Salt: to taste
  • Water: as needed
  • Fresh cilantro: for garnish

Process

  • step1: Rinse the toor dal thoroughly under running water until the water runs clear.
  • step2: Place the rinsed dal in a pressure cooker with 2 1/2 cups of water and 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder.
  • step3: Close the lid and cook for 4-5 whistles or until the dal is soft and mushy.
  • step4: Once the pressure releases naturally, open the lid and mash the dal with a ladle or a masher. Set aside.
  • step5: If using whole tamarind, soak a small lemon-sized ball of tamarind in 1/2 cup of warm water for about 10 minutes.
  • step6: Squeeze the soaked tamarind to extract the juice and strain to remove the pulp and seeds. Set the tamarind juice aside.
  • step7: In a large pot, add the chopped mixed vegetables, chopped tomatoes, chopped onions (if using), and slit green chilies.
  • step8: Pour in enough water to cover the vegetables.
  • step9: Add the tamarind pulp to the pot.
  • step10: Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the vegetables are tender but not mushy.
  • step11: Once the vegetables are cooked, add the mashed toor dal to the pot.
  • step12: Stir in 2 tablespoons of sambar powder and salt to taste.
  • step13: Mix well and adjust the consistency by adding more water if necessary. The sambar should have a slightly thick, pourable consistency.
  • step14: Simmer the sambar on low heat for another 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  • step15: In a small pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium heat.
  • step16: Add 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  • step17: Add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds, 1/2 teaspoon of fenugreek seeds, 2-3 dry red chilies, a pinch of asafoetida, and a few sprigs of curry leaves.
  • step18: Saut√© for a few seconds until the spices become fragrant and the curry leaves are crisp.
  • step19: Pour the prepared tempering over the simmering sambar.
  • step20: Mix well and let the sambar simmer for a few more minutes to incorporate the flavors of the tempering.
  • step21: Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves.

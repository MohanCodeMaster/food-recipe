Recipe Revel




Ingredients

  • Fish fillets: 500 grams (use firm fish like kingfish, salmon, or tilapia)
  • Turmeric powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Red chili powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Coriander powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Cumin powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Garam masala: 1 teaspoon
  • Yogurt: 1/2 cup
  • Lemon juice: 1 tablespoon
  • Ginger-garlic paste: 2 tablespoons
  • Salt: 1 teaspoon
  • Basmati rice: 2 cups
  • Water: 4 cups
  • Bay leaf: 1
  • Cinnamon stick: 1-inch piece
  • Cloves: 4
  • Cardamom pods: 3
  • Salt: 1 teaspoon
  • Onions: 3 large, thinly sliced
  • Tomatoes: 2 medium, chopped
  • Green chilies: 2, slit
  • Ginger-garlic paste: 1 tablespoon
  • Fresh cilantro: 1/4 cup, chopped
  • Mint leaves: 1/4 cup, chopped
  • Oil: 3 tablespoons
  • Ghee: 2 tablespoons
  • Saffron: a pinch, soaked in 2 tablespoons of warm milk (optional)
  • Biryani masala: 1 tablespoon (optional)

Process

  • step1: In a large bowl, combine the yogurt, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, salt, lemon juice, and ginger-garlic paste.
  • step2: Add the fish fillets to the marinade and mix gently to coat all the pieces. Cover and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes.
  • step3: Rinse the basmati rice in water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes.
  • step4:In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the bay leaf, cinnamon stick, cloves, cardamom pods, and salt.
  • step5: Add the soaked rice and cook until the rice is 70-80% cooked (the rice should still have a bite to it). Drain the rice and set aside.
  • step6: Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the marinated fish fillets and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they are just cooked through.
  • step7: Be careful not to overcook the fish. Remove and set aside.
  • step8: In the same pan, add the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil and 2 tablespoons of ghee. Heat over medium heat.
  • step9: Add the thinly sliced onions and fry until they turn golden brown and caramelized. This may take about 10-15 minutes.
  • step10: Remove half of the fried onions and set aside for layering and garnish.
  • step11:Add the ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies to the remaining onions in the pan. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  • step12: Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate from the mixture.
  • step13: Add the turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, and biryani masala (if using). Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  • step14: Add the chopped mint and cilantro. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  • step15:In a heavy-bottomed pot or a large oven-safe dish, layer half of the partially cooked rice at the bottom.
  • step16:Spread the cooked fish fillets evenly over the rice layer.
  • step17: Cover with the remaining rice.
  • step18: Drizzle the saffron milk (if using) and the plain milk over the rice.
  • step19: Top with the reserved fried onions, chopped cilantro, and mint leaves.
  • step20: Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. If the lid is not tight enough, seal the edges with dough to trap the steam.
  • step21:Place the pot on a tava (griddle) over low heat and cook for 20-25 minutes. Alternatively, you can place the pot in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for 25-30 minutes.
  • step22:Once done, remove from heat and let it rest for 10 minutes before opening the lid.
  • step23:Gently fluff the biryani with a fork to mix the layers.
